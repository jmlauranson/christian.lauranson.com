Christian LAURANSON-ROSAZ was born in 1952 in Saône-et-Loire.

He was a student and then an assistant at the Jean Monnet University of Saint-Etienne, and was a lecturer from 1985 to 1996.

He was appointed Professor at the University of Auvergne (Clermont I), where he worked from 1996 to 2002.

From 2002 to 2015, he became a Professor at the University Jean Moulin Lyon 3.

Doctor of State in History of Law in 1984 (University of Paris X-Nanterre), [his thesis](https://www.academia.edu/3513062/LAuvergne_et_ses_marges_Velay_G%C3%A9vaudan_du_VIIIe_au_XIe_si%C3%A8cle_La_fin_du_monde_antique_th%C3%A8se_r%C3%A9%C3%A9dition_2007_{:target="_blank"}), directed by Jean-Pierre POLY, was entitled:
\*\*The Auvergne and its margins (Velay, Gévaudan) from the 8th to the 11th century. The end of the ancient world ?
(Le Puy-en-Velay, Les Cahiers de la Haute-Loire, 1987, published by Librairie internationale Picard, reissued in 2007).

He attempted to demonstrate the survival/permanence of ancient values - those of the Lower Empire - in terms of law and institutions as well as socio-economic structures, culture and mentalities, and the upheaval they experienced around the year 1000, an upheaval brought about by the famous and contested 'feudal mutation'.
